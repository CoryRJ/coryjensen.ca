Swing Dancing

What is swing dancing?

Swing dancing was popular from 1920-1940, though some people still do it today. It mostly includes partner dances like Lindy Hop and East Coast Swing but also has some solo dances like Charleston. Swing dancing uses jazz music, often the Big Band subgenre. All in all, Swing Dancing is a great way to socialize, have fun and to get some exercise.

My experience with swing dancing:

I started swing dancing in 2011 and still do it today, of course I take a break every now and then for a couple months. Swing dancing is a fun way to get your cardio in as you socialize. In many larger cities there is often a swing dance club you can go to, and it is always a friendly environment with welcoming people - at least, in my experience. If you have never tried it, I highly encourage you look up a club near you and give it a try.

Reading

My experience in reading:

I started reading consistently around grade 9. The first fantasy series that became one of my favourites is The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan. It was an amazing series and it gave me a very good impression on the fantasy genre - specifically, it made me love long series, it has 16 books, and the epic fantasy subgenre.

What books do I recommend?

Mistborn, by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson finished off The Wheel of Time when Robert Jordan passed away. After reading the books in the series that he finished, I decided to check out this Brandon guy. One of the first series of his that I read was the Mistborn series. I highly recommend this book, I love the characters even after having read it so long ago. The setting is in a post apocalyptic world after the hero has already lost to the villian.

Assassin's Apprentice, by Robin Hobb

I very recently started reading Robin Hobbs books, and I am not disappointed. This series starts out with a boy being dropped off at a keep. This boy is the illigitamate son of the king in waiting, and is now being taken care of by those who were close to his father. He is eventually brought into the castle and is trained as an assassin. This is a very good series, I actually started out with the later books in the series and you wont be disappointed.



Bouldering

What is bouldering?

In bouldering, you climb on a wall, often slanted in a way that tries to mimic some cliffs, that has man made hand holds drilled into it. There are routes that are color coded in which you must climb through using only the allowed hand holds, with varying levels of difficulty.

My experience with bouldering:

I started bouldering in 2015 at the University of Calgary. It is a very fun way to socialize, get a forearm workout, and an upper body work out if you climb in a more tiring way. There are bouldering walls in most large cities and I recommend that you go and try it out.



Exercising

My experience with exercise:

Since grade 9 I have nearly always tried to stay in shape outside of the hobbies that I do. From grade 9 to university, I typically tried to go to the gym at least twice a week though of course I would take breaks every once in a while. These days, I go and workout about four out of every five days. I recently accomplished two of my fitness goals, those being 40 pull ups in 3 sets and 20 pull ups in 1 set.

My fitness goals:

Human flag for 5 seconds

After having accomplished my previous goals, I discovered I was able to do a poor human flag for 2 seconds. When I found this I out, I have since been working hard at shoulder and core exercises to get the amount of time I can do this up. I have since improved to being able to do a good human flag for about 3 seconds.

Hold a front lever for 5 seconds

A front lever is an exercise where you hold onto a bar that is above you and you lift your torso up so that it is perfectly straight and level with the ground. I am able to hold this for about 5 seconds if I have one leg tucked up into my chest, and am able to raise my body up and hold for 1 with my body straight but knees bent. I am still far from this goal, but I get closer every week.
